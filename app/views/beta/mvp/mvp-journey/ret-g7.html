Find out what your employee needs

You should talk to your employee about how you can help them return to work. They could be happy to return to their current job, or they might need some changes.

You should discuss any changes you can make together. There are a few things you can think about, and no change is right for everyone.

Examples of changes for mental health conditions

Talk to your employee about changes like:

  • having quiet areas in the office
  • different start times or working hours
  • help managing their workload

Examples of changes for physical conditions

Discuss possible changes like:

  • providing wheelchair access
  • giving them a parking space near to work
  • software for people with visual impairments

More help with employee’s return

The Access to Work scheme can help your employee with the costs of some changes. It can also provide a workplace assessment to decide which changes are needed.

An occupational health expert can be useful if the case is complex or you're not sure what to do. ACAS has information on using occupational health at work.

There is more information about using occupational health at workon the ACAS website.
