Dismiss someone fairly

As a last resort, you can dismiss someone if you cannot make changes to accommodate them. But only based on their ability to do their job.

Fair dismissals involving a health condition or disability

If your employee cannot do their job because no reasonable adjustments can be made, it may be fair for you to dismiss them, even if they’re disabled.

For a dismissal to be fair, you must have a good reason and behave reasonably.

Before you dismiss someone with a heath condition or disability, you might want to ensure you have:

  • talked to your employee about their condition
  • tried to make changes to help them stay at work
  • asked for an occupational health assessment or medical report

See GOV.UK guidance on dismissing staff (opens in a new tab)

Capability procedures

It’s important to investigate the situation and make sure your employee is no longer able to do their job.

ACAS has information on capability procedures (opens in a new tab) that can help you to do this.
