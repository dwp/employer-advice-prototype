What to do if your employee has not told you about a disability or health condition

It can be helpful for you to know why your employee is off work, but they do not have to tell you.

It's important to create a culture where employees are happy to talk about about their health.

Avoid judgements when you talk about disabilities or health conditions. This is especially important for mental health conditions.

Explain that you will keep health information private, and only use it to make changes to help them do their job.

When you can ask for medical evidence

You can ask for medical evidence if your employee is off work for 8 days or more. Examples of medical evidence are a letter from a hospital or a fit note from a doctor.

A fit note will have a summary of the disability or health condition and a recommendation about your employee’s ability to work.

If your employee does not give you medical evidence after 8 days’ absence you do not have to pay Statutory Sick Pay.
