Tips on talking to your employee

It's important to talk to your employee as soon as you can. These topics can help you have an open and useful conversation.

Review the following prompts. You may want to add notes as you discuss things with your employee.

You can return to this website later for more advice on supporting your employee by making any changes they need.



Ask about them first

Start by asking about your employee in general - find out how they're doing.

"Thanks for letting me know..."

"How are you in general?"



Talk about a plan to keep in touch

Agree when you will get in touch next, and how often you will speak. Choose a method of communication that works for you both.

"When should we talk again?"



Your employee's situation

You can start to ask about your employee's experience, and share anything you have already learned.

"What are you comfortable sharing right now?"

"Here's what I have found out, does that apply to you?"



Job and workplace

When it feels appropriate, talk about how the job or work environment might be making things difficult. You could arrange a time to talk about support in more detail.

"How is work making things difficult?"

"When you're ready we can talk about changes..."



Check in with your employee as often as works for you both. We can email a copy of this document to your email address if you would like to save or print it.
