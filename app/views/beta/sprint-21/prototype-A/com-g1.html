Stay in touch with your employee

You can contact your employee while they are off work. Starting a positive conversation can make your employee feel supported and able to return.

What to say to your employee

Let your employee know you support them. You should only talk to them about work when they are ready.

You could say:

  • "take some time to look after yourself"
  • "let us know if there is anything you need"
  • "can we arrange a time to talk about work and agree next steps?"

What not to say

Try not to put pressure on your employee. Focus on the support they might need. If they have a disability or long-term condition, take care to ask if their absence is related and what that means for them.

You should not say:

  • "have you done this task?"
  • "when will things be back to normal?"
  • "we need to get you back as quickly as possible"

How to stay in touch

There are no rules about how often you get in touch. You and your employee should agree how often you will contact each other.

Use whatever method feels most appropriate for you both. For example, it could be an email, phone call or text message.
