Have regular meetings with your employee

It's important to keep checking that your employee feels supported and any changes are still right for them.

Keep talking with your employee

Talk regularly to your employee about what they need. The changes you put in place now might not always be right for them.

Some conditions will vary over the long term, and others will have good and bad days. Try to accommodate this kind of change.

Record and update a plan

You should keep a record of the changes or reasonable adjustments you have agreed. Review it together regularly.

A reasonable adjustment plan is useful for this. The Business Disability Forum (opens in new tab) has guidance on reasonable adjustment plans.

Support from occupational health

If the changes you put in place are not supporting your employee as much as they should, or if the situation has changed, you might find it useful to get advice from an occupational health provider.

Find more about using occupational health services at Acas (the Advisory, Conciliation and Arbitration service - opens in new tab).
