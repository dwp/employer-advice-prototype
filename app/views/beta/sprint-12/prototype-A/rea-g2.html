Understanding disability

The law defines disability as a physical or mental health condition that has a ‘substantial’ and ‘long-term’ negative effect on someone’s ability to do normal daily activities.

To understand what ‘substantial’ and ‘long-term’ mean, see the definition of disability under the Equality Act 2010 (opens in a new tab).

How to know if your employee is disabled

Not all disabilities are visible, and you might not know if your employee is disabled.

You’re allowed to ask if your employee is disabled. If you ask, be clear that you’re asking because you want to support them.

Your employee does not have to tell you if they’re disabled.
