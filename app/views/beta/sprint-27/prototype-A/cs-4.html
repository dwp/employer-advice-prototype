Case study 4

Version A

Rachel is Maître D in a busy hotel restaurant. It’s a high pressure role and she represents the restaurant in the customer-facing position. She started menopause a couple of years ago.

Rachel’s symptoms have developed recently. She is finding it difficult to concentrate. She sometimes forgets customer requests and requirements, and has difficulty remembering menu and wine pairing changes.

She has worked at the restaurant for many years but her increasing anxiety levels and tiredness mean that she has begun questioning her future there.

The restaurant and kitchen are already hot, and her role means that Rachel is always on her feet. When a hot flush comes it can be very uncomfortable and embarrassing, especially if she is dealing with a customer complaint.

She feels there is no time to take extra breaks to freshen up or to try to manage the flushes privately.

Rachel has not discussed her symptoms with the restaurant manager as he is just starting out his career in the industry and she does not want to bother him.

Rachel mentions in a conversation with the head server that she has been very tired lately and is embarrassed about sweating in front of customers.

The head server agrees to cover the maitre d duties when needed but it must be agreed with the manager. Rachel tells her manager that this will be happening for a trial period.

The restaurant manager researches menopause and sets up an awareness training session for all staff to understand customer needs, as well as possible staff requirements.

It’s agreed that Rachel will work more closely with the head server and will take rest breaks when they are needed.

Version B

Rachel is Maître D in a busy restaurant and is going through menopause.

This means:

  • it is difficult for Rachel to concentrate in the noisy and busy environment
  • it makes her very uncomfortable and embarrassed during a hot flush
  • it increases her anxiety when dealing with customers
  • she is forgetting menu changes

Rachel meets with the head server

They discuss options available to them to manage the symptoms at work including:

  • the head server covering Maître D duties when Rachel needs to freshen up or take a quick break
  • Rachel will let the restaurant manager know about how symptoms are affecting her role

Rachel meets with her manager

It’s agreed that:

  • for a trial period the head server will cover maitre d duties when needed
  • Rachel’s manager will research menopause to get a greater understanding
  • all staff will attend a menopause awareness training course to understand customer needs, as well as possible staff requirements

Rachel has regular catchups with her manager to discuss how she is managing her menopause.

Version C

Rachel is Maître D in a busy restaurant and is going through menopause.

She is anxious and embarrassed about some of her symptoms and this is affecting her customer-facing role.

Rachel agrees with her manager that, for a trial period, other staff can cover her duties when she needs a quick break during service. All staff attend a menopause awareness course.

They catch up regularly to check the symptoms are being managed effectively.
