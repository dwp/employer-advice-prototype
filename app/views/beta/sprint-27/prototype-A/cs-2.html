Case study 2

Version A

Ashley works at a gym. They have been nervous about coming in for their shifts following an argument with a very angry customer who used threatening language and behaviour.

Ashley’s manager notices that they're not as welcoming or chatty with customers as they were, are avoiding eye contact when working the front desk and no longer volunteers to lead classes

After a discussion with their manager, Ashley admits to being nervous and anxious following the attack. They love their job, but don't feel comfortable working alone or facing customers.

Their manager suggests Ashley takes a break from the front desk for a couple of weeks to work on securing and checking equipment and supplies.

It's also suggested that all staff work in a pairs when opening up and closing the gym, so no one is ever working on their own.

They agree to meet in a couple of weeks to see how the anxiety levels are following the change in working. Ashley now feels comfortable observing classes and is working towards leading a session in a couple of weeks. They're also working with a buddy on the front desk one shift a week for a trail period.

Version B

A gym employee was verbally attacked by a customer at the gym they work at.

Following the attack they:

  • feel nervous about coming in for their shifts
  • are not as welcoming or chatty to customers
  • no longer make eye contact with customers
  • no longer volunteer to lead gym classes

After a discussion with their manager

The employer suggests the employee:

  • takes a break from customer-facing tasks
  • works on securing and checking equipment and supplies

It’s also suggested that all staff work in pairs when opening and locking up the gym.

They agree to meet in a couple of weeks following the change in working.

The employee now feels comfortable observing classes and is working towards leading a session in a couple of weeks. They're also working with a buddy on the front desk one shift per week for a trial period.

Version C

Ashley was verbally attacked in the gym where they work. This triggered an existing anxiety condition.

Their manager and team notice their change in behaviour.

They suggest moving away from customer facing tasks for a couple of weeks.

Ashley’s confidence grows and they are ready to lead classes again and work with customers.
