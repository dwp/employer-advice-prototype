Consider occupational health support

Occupational health professionals can help you to manage health and wellbeing at work.

How they can help

Occupational health experts can help with employee health by:

  • providing health assessments for your employees
  • helping you decide on any changes you might need to make at work
  • supporting your employees to manage their health

They also prepare your business for any future issues by:

  • helping you monitor the health of your employees
  • managing cases over time and supporting employees to return to work
  • helping you to create policies for your business

These services will need to be paid for, but can save you time and money by helping your employees to stay healthy and productive. Teams might include people like doctors, nurses, counsellors and assessors.

You can find more information about using occupational health services (opens in a new tab) from the ACAS website.

Finding occupational health services

The following links open in a new tab.

The Society of Occupational Medicine is a membership body with a tool to help you find a local provider. The Commercial Occupational Health Providers’ Association also has a contact form for local providers.

The Safe Effective Quality Occupational Health Service (SEQOHS) maintains quality standards and has a list of services which meet their criteria.

The NHS Health and Work Network represents occupational health teams in the NHS. It also provides support to business and a map of local NHS providers.
