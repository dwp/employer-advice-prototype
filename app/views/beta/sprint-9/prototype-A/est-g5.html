What to do if your employee does not want to tell you about their disability or health condition

It can be helpful for you to know why your employee is off work, but they do not have to tell you.

You can help create a culture in your workplace that encourages employees to share relevant health information with you.

Explain to employees how you keep health information private, and only use it to make positive changes to help them do their job. Be non-judgemental when you talk about disabilities or health conditions, and encourage colleagues to do the same. This is especially important for mental health conditions.

When you can ask for medical evidence

If your employee is off work due to a disability or health condition for 8 or more days you can ask for medical evidence. Examples of medical evidence are a letter from a hospital, or a fit note from a doctor. Fit notes used to be called sick notes.

A fit note will usually include a brief summary of the disability or health condition, and the medical professional’s recommendation about the employee’s ability to work.

If your employee does not give you medical evidence after 8 days’ absence you do not have to pay Statutory Sick Pay.
