Decide what is reasonable for your business

You must make 'reasonable adjustments' to make sure workers with disabilities, or physical or mental health conditions, are not substantially disadvantaged when doing their jobs.

What is considered ‘reasonable’ will depend on your business.

For example, if an employee uses a wheelchair and your workplace does not have space for a lift, you would not be forced to change premises. However, you would be expected to consider other ways for the employee to be able to do their role – such as working from somewhere else.
