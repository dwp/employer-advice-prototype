What to do when your employee cannot return to work

Dismissing someone legally

Dismissing an employee because they cannot work due to long-term illness should only be a final resort. It is usually more cost-effective to retain an employee wherever possible, rather than dismissing them and recruiting and training a new employee.

You must use a fair and reasonable procedure if you dismiss someone.

Avoiding unlawful discrimination

It is possible to dismiss someone who is disabled, but you must not discriminate against them because of their disability. This is unlawful. Your employee could make a claim to an employment tribunal if they believe they were unfairly dismissed.

The Equality Act 2010 defines disability as ‘a physical or mental impairment which has a substantial and long-term adverse effect on a person’s ability to carry out normal day-to- day activities’.

Diagnosing disability can be complex, and you might not know if your employee is disabled.

Find more information about dismissing someone fairly on the ACAS website.
