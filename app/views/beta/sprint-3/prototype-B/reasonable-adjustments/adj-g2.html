Making changes for an employee with a disability or health condition

Discuss possible changes together

You should ask your employee which changes at work might help them. You can make suggestions, but it is important to listen and let them take the lead.

Talk to your employee about possible changes such as:

  • changes to the workplace, or anywhere they work
  • different ways of working
  • giving them extra support

These changes are called reasonable adjustments. This means you should make any changes if they are reasonable and affordable for your business.

More about reasonable adjustments on GOV.UK

Help with reasonable adjustments

Help with costs might be available if you cannot make the changes your employee needs.

Access to Work is a free government service where your employee can apply for help with the costs of changes. They can also provide a workplace assessment to decide which changes are needed.

If your employee is disabled

The law says that you must make reasonable adjustments if your employee is a disabled person. You must take care not to discriminate against them.
