Guidance on recruiting and onboarding employees in an inclusive way

You should hire the best person for the job based on their application and interview.

You must avoid disability discrimination during the recruitment and selection process. Applicants are protected under the Equality Act 2010. Read more about the Equality Act 2010 on GOV.UK (opens in new tab).

You can ask applicants if they need any reasonable adjustments. Read more about reasonable adjustments on GOV.UK (opens in new tab).

Find out about the benefits of recruiting people with health conditions and disabilities on GOV.UK (opens in new tab)

These resources could help you to become a more inclusive employer, from advertising the role, to interviewing and onboarding.

Resources for job adverts and interviewing

The guidance on GOV.UK (opens in new tab) could help you to encourage applications from disabled people and avoid discrimination in job adverts.

  • Check the guide to inclusive recruitment on CIPD website. (opens in new tab)
  • AbilityNet offer a course on accessible, inclusive recruitment (opens in new tab)
  • Checking how Business Disability Forum members (opens in new tab) word their own job adverts could help you when advertising for your roles

Resources for preventing discrimination and making adjustments

  • Find out more about preventing discrimination on GOV.UK. (opens in new tab)
  • Watch a video on how an employer adapted working arrangement for their employee with Motor neurone disease
  • Employers can sign up for the Disability Confident scheme (opens in new tab)

Resources for inclusive onboarding

  • Find out more about inclusive and accessible onboarding on the AbilityNet website (opens in new tab)

Resources for financial assistance

  • Your employee could apply for Access to Work (opens in new tab)
